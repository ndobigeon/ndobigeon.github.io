Ph.D. and postdoctoral positions

in machine learning and signal & image processing

Starting date: Fall 2020

Description

Within the Artificial and Natural Intelligence Toulouse Institute (3IA ANITI, Toulouse, France), several Ph.D. and postdoctoral positions are open. Areas of interest include (but are not limited to):

  • machine learning, representation learning, deep learning
  • computational statistics (e.g., Monte Carlo methods), optimization
  • signal and image processing, computational imaging
  • pattern recognition, computer vision

with applications to

  • Earth observation and remote sensing
  • smart and digital agriculture
  • astronomy and astrophysics
  • microscopy and material sciences
  • medical imaging and digital health
  • data mining and recommendation systems

For instance, subjects of interest deal with

  • deep generative models for Monte Carlo sampling
  • change detection for multitemporal multisource remote sensing data
  • machine learning for remote sensing & Earth observation (as part of the ai4Geo researh program)

These topics can be adjusted according to the candidate profile (see below).

The monthly gross salaries are 2600 euros for a Ph.D. position and start from 3500 euros for a postdoctoral position. The positions come with health insurance and other social benefits.

Context

The open positions are part of a AI Research Chair lead by Nicolas Dobigeon (IUF & Toulouse INP) within the Artificial and Natural Intelligence Toulouse Institute (ANITI, Toulouse, France). Associate members are Cédric Févotte (CNRS, IRIT), Thomas Oberlin (Toulouse INP, IRIT), Mathieu Fauvel (INRA, CESBIO) and Jordi Inglada (CNES, CESBIO).

Candidate profile

Prospective applicants should have a strong mathematical background (probability & statistics, optimization, etc.), good scientific programming skills and good communication skills in English, both written and oral, with particular interests in one or several aforementioned disciplines.

Application

To apply for a Ph.D. thesis, you are invited to provide (as pdf files)

  • a detailed curriculum,
  • up to three recommendation letters stating your ability for research,
  • official transcripts from each institution you have attended (in French or English).

To apply for a post-doctoral position You are invited to provide (as pdf files)

  • a detailed curriculum, including a list of publications,
  • up to three recommendation letters stating your ability for research,
  • a short research statement.

Applications will be collected by email (nicolas.dobigeon@enseeiht.fr) until suitable candidates are found.

Given the large number of applications I receive, I am unable to individually respond to all emails from prospective students/fellows who wish to join our research group. If we are interested in your candidacy, we will contact you promptly. If you do not receive any response, no need to send several reminders.
